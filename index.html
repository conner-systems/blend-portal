The 12-Month Clock — How It Works
The 12-month commission clock is tied to each individual location — not to the rep's overall tenure. Every machine installed starts its own independent clock.
A rep who places Location A in January earns 5% on Location A through January of next year. When they place Location B in June, Location B starts its own 5% clock running through June of next year. Location A transitions to 2% in January while Location B is still earning 5%.
The result: a rep who keeps placing new locations always has a mix of 5% (new) and 2% (mature) accounts. Their blended rate stays strong as long as they keep growing. The model rewards continuous placement — not just an initial burst of activity.

Clawback Structure
The placement commission is split into two payments — $750 at install and $750 at the 90-day review — for a specific reason. A qualifying 90-day review requires the location to average 25+ orders per day over the 90-day period.
A rep who earns the full commission only after proving the location performs has every incentive to place the right machine in the right location. A rep who earns everything at signing has an incentive to place machines everywhere and move on regardless of fit.
The 90-day review also triggers the optional $500 performance bonus — meaning a rep who places a great location earns $2,000 total in placement commissions versus $750 for a location that doesn't hit the performance threshold. Quality placements are worth 2.67x more than marginal ones.

Happy Hour + Power Hour — Six Hours of Strategic Incentives
Happy Hour runs twice per day during the weakest traffic hours. Each session is two hours long — four hours total per day. Timing is configured per location from traffic data; a typical gym runs 11:30am–1:30pm (post–morning-rush lull) and 7:00–9:00pm (evening wind-down). A push notification is sent 15 minutes before each window opens.
Happy Hour runs at 20% off — a percentage discount, not a fixed dollar amount. This protects margin across every price point on the menu. A $2.75 electrolyte water at 20% off becomes $2.20. A $10.00 performance blend at 20% off becomes $8.00. The discount scales with the drink — members at every tier get a meaningful incentive without the machine giving away margin on low-cost drinks.
Power Hour runs twice per day during the strongest traffic hours. Each session is one hour long — two hours total per day. This is not a discount: members earn double Blend Bucks on every purchase, or receive a free supplement add-on with every drink (location-configurable). A typical gym runs 6:30–7:30am (morning rush) and 5:00–6:00pm (evening rush). A push notification is sent when Power Hour starts. The purpose is to reward the most engaged members at peak moments and increase loyalty and order attachment rate when the floor is already busy.
Discount when it is slow to drive volume. Reward when it is busy to drive loyalty. Never discount at peak — that is what Power Hour is for.

Streak System — Daily Habit Formation
A streak is the number of consecutive days a member orders from BLEND+. Streaks are displayed prominently on the home screen — a visible counter that creates loss aversion. Missing a day resets the streak, which is a psychologically significant loss for a member who has maintained a 14-day run.
  • Streak display — prominent on home screen, shown as a flame icon with day count. Makes the streak feel like an achievement worth protecting.
  • Streak Shield — purchasable with Blend Bucks, protects the streak for one missed day. Creates a secondary spend behavior and softens churn from travel or illness.
  • Streak milestones — bonus BB awards at 7, 14, 30, and 60-day streaks. Each milestone is a push notification moment — drives social sharing behavior.
  • Streak leaderboard — optional, per-location. Members can see where they rank among their gym's BLEND+ users. Competitive environments (CrossFit gyms, college teams) respond strongly to leaderboard mechanics.

Why a Delaware C-Corp and not an LLC?
The SAFE converts into preferred stock at the next priced round. Preferred stock only exists in a corporation — not an LLC. Every investor-backed startup uses a Delaware C-Corp for this reason. The Bonars' attorney will expect it and the YC SAFE template requires it.

If LOIs don't materialize, Tranche 3 does not release. You are not exposed to $100,000 of capital on a machine that hasn't proven market demand.
